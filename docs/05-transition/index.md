# Transition

The FO transition spans from the election (July Council meeting) through the
first month of the new executive taking office (August 31). Outgoing and
incoming FOs share responsibility for a smooth handoff.

**Incoming FO** takes office August 31. They may begin receiving transition
honoraria two weeks before that date (Bylaw 8.6.2).

**Outgoing FO** may receive transition honoraria for one month after vacating
office, subject to completing the requirements below (Bylaw 8.6.2).

---

## Transition Honorarium Structure (Executive Policy Appendix I)

The Executive Oversight Committee (EOC), in consultation with the incoming FO
and GM, determines what portion of the transition honorarium the outgoing FO
receives, based on completion of five components:

| Component | Weight | What it includes |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Comprehensive transitional report | **40%** | Committees sat on; student staff positions; media issues; successes/failures; upcoming deadlines |
| Training of incoming member | **15%** | Staff introductions; day-to-day task familiarization; attendance at ≥ 3 Standing Committee meetings; instruction on cheque signing procedures |
| Consultation after incoming takes office | **15%** | Remaining available for at least 1 month to answer questions and flag outstanding issues |
| Organization of office and files | **15%** | Organize computer and paper files; remove personal belongings; return GSS equipment; ensure all access is transferred; clean office |
| Assisting in position reviews | **15%** | Participate in reviews for any position that the incoming FO requests |

{: .note }
The transitional report must be a new document focused on the current year —
not a copy of a prior year's report (Executive Policy Appendix I).

If the outgoing FO is re-elected to the same position, they do not receive a
transition honorarium (Executive Policy 5.3).
