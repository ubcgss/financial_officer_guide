# Files and Records

The GSS maintains files in Dropbox (primary document store) and Notion
(workspace / project tracking). The outgoing FO is responsible for
organizing all files before handing off (Executive Policy Appendix I,
Organization of office and files — 15% of transition honorarium).

---

## Dropbox Folder Structure

Primary path: `Dropbox/Work/UBC GSS/`

Within that path, the FO's relevant folders typically include:

```
Work/UBC GSS/
├── Financial Officer/          ← FO-specific working documents
│   ├── [Fiscal Year]/
│   │   ├── Budget/
│   │   ├── Financial Reports/
│   │   ├── HFC Packages/
│   │   └── Council Presentations/
├── GSS Policy Manuals/         ← Official policy documents
├── Shared — GM + FO/           ← Documents shared with GM
└── Transition/                 ← Transition reports and handoff materials
```

Verify the exact structure with the GM, as it may have evolved. The outgoing
FO should organize and label all folders before August 31.

---

## Notion Workspace

The GSS uses Notion for project tracking and internal documentation. Confirm
with the GM what Notion pages the FO maintains access to and ensure the
incoming FO is invited before handoff.

---

## What to Archive vs. Hand Off Actively

| Item | Action |
|------|--------|
| Completed monthly financial reports (prior years) | Archive in Dropbox under the relevant fiscal year |
| Current fiscal year financial reports | Ensure in Dropbox and briefed to incoming FO |
| HFC packages and minutes | Archive in Dropbox; flag any outstanding action items |
| Signed budget (current year) | Hand off actively with briefing |
| Ongoing vendor/supplier correspondence | Hand off actively; brief incoming FO |
| Pending cheque requisitions | Resolve before handoff or flag as outstanding |
| Access credentials / login info | Do not store passwords in files — coordinate transfers through system admins (GM) |
| Physical files / office materials | Remove personal items; organize and label remaining files; clean office space per EOC direction |
