# House-Finance Committee (HFC)

The FO is the responsible executive for the House-Finance Committee (Bylaw
8.5.6(e)). The HFC is responsible for reviewing and overseeing all GSS
financial matters.

---

## Composition

The HFC is a Standing Committee of the GSS. Per HF Policy 3.8(a), no voting
member of the HFC may be an employee of the Society (though employees may
participate in discussion). Confirm the current HFC membership and quorum
requirements with the GM; these are set out in the GSS Committee and Caucus
Policy.

---

## Meeting Cadence

HFC meets monthly to review GSS financial transactions, budgets, and any
financial policy matters. Coordinate meeting scheduling with the GM and HFC
Chair. The HFC may request further information or clarification from the GM,
Accountant, and FO at any meeting.

---

## The FO's Role in HFC

As responsible executive, you:
- Present the monthly profit and loss statements and budget-to-actuals summary.
- Present the annual budget to HFC before it goes to Council.
- Facilitate the annual review of the House-Finance Policy (HF Policy 1.3).
- Report any emergency expenditures to the next HFC meeting (HF Policy 3.9(d)).
- Work with the GM to manage operations within the approved budgets, subject
  to special expenses approved by HFC and emergency expenses necessary to
  avoid immediate substantial harm to the Society.

---

## Preparing HFC Packages

A standard HFC package contains:
1. Agenda (prepared by GM or HFC Chair)
2. Monthly profit and loss statement (prepared by Bookkeeper/Accountant)
3. Budget-to-actuals report for each department
4. Any items requiring HFC approval (e.g., unbudgeted expenditures, budget
   reallocations, new contracts)
5. Minutes from the previous HFC meeting (for approval)

**Submission deadline:** The FO and GM must submit the budget and P&L
statements to HFC **within five weeks of the end of the month to be reviewed**
(HF Policy 3.9(b)).

---

## Emergency Expenditures

The FO and GM may approve emergency expenses necessary to avoid immediate,
substantial adverse effects on the Society — even without prior HFC approval.
Any such emergency expense must be reported to the **next HFC meeting**
(HF Policy 3.9(d)).
