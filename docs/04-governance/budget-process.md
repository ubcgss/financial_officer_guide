# Budget Process

The GSS annual budget covers one fiscal year (June 1–May 31). The budget
preparation process runs **February through May**, with the incoming executive
team working on the budget for their term during the transition period.

---

## February–May Timeline

(Source: HF Policy Appendix II, Financial Budget Guideline)

### February
- Accountant, HFC, GM, and FO review, discuss, and alter (if needed) the
  existing budget notes.
- GM and FO send out a **new budget timeline** to all executives.
- GM and FO meet outgoing executives for budget feedback and suggestions for
  the next year's budget.
- GM and FO send out a **new budget line template** to all departments.
- FO participates in the election's all-candidate meeting.

### March
- FO and GM meet elected (incoming) executives to ascertain their preliminary
  goals and how budgetary allocations would be affected.

### April
- Elected executives provide **first departmental draft budgets** to HFC
  (mid-April).
- HFC commences review of executive budgets.
- Elected executives provide **second departmental draft budgets** to HFC by
  end of April.

### May
- HFC reviews and discusses the second departmental draft budgets.
- Executives make recommended changes.
- HFC approves the final master budget **at least one week before** the May
  Council meeting.
- FO presents the final master budget at the May Council meeting.
- Master budget is approved at the May Council meeting.

## Five-Department Structure

The GSS budget is a master budget comprising five departmental sub-budgets:

| Code | Department | Responsible executive | Budget type |
|------|-----------|----------------------|------------|
| **10-CORP** | Corporate | President, GM, FO | Revenue + expenditure |
| **11-PRES** | President | President | Expenditure |
| **12-VPUAA** | VP University and Academic Affairs | VP UAA | Expenditure |
| **13-VPEXT** | VP External Relations | VP External | Expenditure |
| **14-VPSTD** | VP Students | VP Students | Revenue + expenditure |

**10-CORP major revenues:** Membership fees, Sauder lease charges, Koerner's
Pub space rental charges, bookings space rental charges.

**10-CORP major expenses:** Staff salaries and benefits, executives'
honoraria, professional service charges, commercial rent to UBC, marketing
costs.

**14-VPSTD major revenues:** Programs, events, orientation sales.

## Budget Reallocation

During the fiscal year, reallocations between budget lines may be made by
the FO in consultation with the Executives and the HFC. Such redistributions
**must be reported to Council**.

---

## Budgeting Principles

1. **Provide realistic estimates.** Budget padding (underestimating revenue or
   overestimating costs) is not permitted. Each executive is accountable for
   accurate projections and must explain variances when comparing actuals to
   budget.

2. **Standardize data and processes.** Use the standard template provided by
   the GM and Accountant. The GSS uses Microsoft Excel for budgeting.

3. **Accountability and accessibility.** Each department head must be prepared
   to explain variances between actuals and budget at every monthly review.
