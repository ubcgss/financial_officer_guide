# Investment Accounts

The GSS holds investment accounts comprising **Guaranteed Investment
Certificates (GICs)** and **Mutual Funds**. These accounts hold funds that
are not required in the short term.

---

## Purpose

Investment accounts allow the GSS to earn returns on reserves that are not
needed for immediate operations. The mix of GICs (fixed-term, fixed-rate) and
Mutual Funds (market-linked) reflects the Society's Statement of Investment
Policy (SIP).

---

## Investment Manager Transition

{: .note }
As of the guide's last update, the GSS is in the process of transitioning its
investment manager to **RBC Dominion Securities**. Verify the current status
of this transition with the GM before taking any action on investment accounts.

---

## Statement of Investment Policy (SIP)

The SIP sets the guidelines for how the GSS invests its reserves, including
risk tolerance, asset allocation limits, and reporting requirements. Verify
the current SIP with the GM.

---

## Annual Review

Conduct an annual review of the investment portfolio in consultation with the
GM and the investment manager. The review should assess:
- Current portfolio composition vs. SIP targets
- Returns for the year
- Whether any GICs are maturing and need to be reinvested or transferred to
  operations

Coordinate the review timing with the GM. Findings should be reported to
the HFC.
