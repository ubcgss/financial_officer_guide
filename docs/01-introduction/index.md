# Introduction to the Financial Officer Role

The Financial Officer (FO) is one of five Executive Officers of the Graduate
Student Society of UBC Vancouver. The FO is elected at the July Council
meeting and takes office on August 31st.

---

## Formal Duties (Bylaw 8.5.6)

The FO shall:

**(a)** Report to Council on the financial situation of the Society on a
monthly basis.

**(b)** Prepare and present a complete budget and financial statements for the
Society each trimester, and ensure the completion of the Society budget process
on schedule with the House-Finance Committee (HFC).

**(c)** Cause an audit of the financial records and books of accounts to be
performed at the end of each fiscal year.

**(d)** Be responsible for the supervision of staff of the Society in financial
matters, and ensure the Society's policy on Signing Authority is followed.

**(e)** Be responsible for the activities of the House-Finance Committee.

**(f)** Ensure the implementation of Bylaw 3.9 and Bylaw 11.

**(g)** Provide at least **thirteen (13) hours of work per week**, including no
less than **three (3) hours of office presence per week**; the time shall be
used to perform normal duties of the position.

**(h)** Report to Council and the President on a regular basis, work
collaboratively with the Executive Committee, and perform such other duties as
directed by Council or the membership in consultation with the President or the
Executive Committee.

---

## Additional Duties (Executive Policy 3.5)

In addition to the Bylaw duties, the FO shall:

**(a)** Ensure the Society's policy on Signing Authority is followed.

**(b)** Ensure the Society's policies on Executive Officer Spending are
followed.

---

## Relationships

| Person / Body | Your relationship |
|---------------|------------------|
| **General Manager (GM)** | Day-to-day operational partner. The GM handles accounts receivable/payable processing, bank deposits, payroll coordination, and reporting to the Accountant and FO. You review; the GM executes. |
| **Accountant (Vistax)** | Prepares monthly financial statements and the annual budget template; conducts and reviews reconciliations. Primary contact for the audit process. |
| **Bookkeeper (Bickert Management)** | Enters transactions into Zoho Books; prepares payroll via Payworks; completes monthly reconciliations and forwards to Accountant and FO. |
| **House-Finance Committee (HFC)** | You are the responsible executive for HFC. The FO chairs or co-chairs the committee, presents monthly financial reports and the annual budget for HFC review before Council. |
| **Council** | You present the monthly financial update at the Council meeting (3rd Thursday of each month, except December where it is the 2nd Thursday). |

---

## Signing Authority

All cheques require two of the three signing officers to approve:

- President
- Financial Officer
- Vice-President, University and Academic Affairs (VP UAA)

The FO's debit card grants authority to withdraw and transfer funds on behalf
of the GSS. The President's debit card is for information and verification
purposes only.

During the FO's vacation, the VP UAA takes over FO responsibilities. If both
the President and VP UAA are away simultaneously, the GSS financial process
proceeds without their approval.

---

## Time Commitment

- **Minimum:** 13 hours per week, including at least 3 hours in-office
  (Bylaw 8.5.6(g))
- **Election:** Elected at the July Council meeting; takes office August 31st
- **Term:** The FO is ineligible to run for any other executive office in the
  GSS at the subsequent election if they hold the position after the December
  Council meeting prior to an executive election. The FO must announce intent
  to resign no later than two weeks before December Council to contest another
  position (Bylaw 8.2.2).

---

## Honorarium and Vacation

**Honorarium:** $17,777.77 per annum (Bylaw 8.6.1(b)), paid in 24
semi-monthly installments, provided all duties are performed to Council's
satisfaction.

**Transition honorarium:** Payable subject to completing: comprehensive
transitional report (40%), training of incoming executive (15%), consultation
after handoff (15%), organization of files (15%), position reviews (15%).
See [Transition](../05-transition/) for details.

**Vacation:** 2 weeks per term, not including the winter closure period as
defined by the UBC Academic Calendar (Executive Policy 4.1). You must notify
the Chair of the Executive Oversight Committee (EOC) of vacation dates before
departing.

---

## What the FO Does Not Own

- **Day-to-day operations** — the GM manages operational staff, room bookings,
  accounts receivable, and payroll execution.
- **Audit preparation** — the Accountant and Bookkeeper prepare the working
  papers; the FO's duty is to *cause* the audit to happen (Bylaw 8.5.6(c)).
- **GSS Event Fund administration** — this is owned by the VP Students
  (Executive Policy 3.2(a)); the FO signs off on fund disbursements but does
  not administer applications.
- **GSFA / Housing Aid** — administered by VP UAA (Executive Policy 3.3(a)).
- **Hiring decisions** — the President and HR Committee set pay rates and
  contract terms; the GM maintains personnel files. New hires and terminations
  must be signed off by the President and FO.
